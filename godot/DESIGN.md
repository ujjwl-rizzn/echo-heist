# ECHO HEIST Reborn Design Bible

## Core Fantasy

You are a thief inside a virtual/physical hybrid heist space where every job can be approached from two ideologies.

The **government spy** steals to expose, disable, or prevent. The spy wins by leaving clean evidence chains, avoiding civilian damage, and extracting with proof.

The **criminal heister** steals to profit, humiliate rivals, or gain power. The heister wins by taking the biggest score, improvising through chaos, and escaping before the world closes in.

Both sides are playable. They share locations, but the mission goals, dialogue, tools, scoring, and endings change.

## View And Feel

The chosen direction is **2.5D cinematic heist view**.

This does not mean a flat side-scroller. The camera should feel staged and deep:

- Angled rooms with foreground and background layers.
- Cover objects that imply depth.
- Guards moving across upper/lower lanes.
- Glass walls, elevators, vents, server floors, rooftops, train cars, vault chambers.
- Big readable silhouettes, like a premium mobile action-stealth game.

The view should support stealth, fighting, shooting, parkour-like movement, puzzles, and escape routes without making the player tiny on phones.

## Two-Side System

### Government Spy

Identity: black-ops infiltrator, clean extraction, surgical precision.

Tools:

- Signal jammer
- Non-lethal takedown
- Evidence scanner
- Camera loop
- Disguise pass
- Silent drone

Scoring:

- Low casualties
- Evidence recovered
- No public exposure
- Clean escape
- Optional truth reveal

### Criminal Heister

Identity: elite underworld thief, bold moves, cash and reputation.

Tools:

- EMP puck
- Loud breach
- Bribe chip
- Smoke blade
- Counterfeit access card
- Rival bait

Scoring:

- Loot value
- Escape style
- Rival humiliation
- Chaos survived
- Optional betrayal bonus

## Mission Shape

Each mission should have:

1. Intel phase: learn the layout or target.
2. Entry phase: choose route.
3. Theft phase: solve the lock, steal the asset.
4. Heat phase: alarms, guards, lockdown, chase.
5. Escape phase: reach extraction.
6. Consequence phase: spy/heister result and score.

## First Level Concept

**The Mirror Archive**

A government-owned data museum is secretly storing stolen identities. The spy version steals proof to expose the program. The heister version steals identity keys to sell to the highest bidder.

The level has:

- Lobby scanner
- Glass security lane
- Server vault
- Guard patrol balcony
- Vent bypass
- Locked archive door
- Escape elevator

## Mobile Rule

Phones are not an afterthought.

The screen should reserve:

- Top strip for score, heat, objective, pause.
- Middle majority for the game view.
- Bottom command deck for joystick and actions.

No tiny player. No giant text cards covering the mission. No desktop layout squeezed into portrait.
